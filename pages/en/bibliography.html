Why Primary and Secondary Bibliographies?




Primary bibliographies comprise sources created solely by the author or poet, free from external intervention. Essentially, they are the author's original works.
Secondary bibliographies, on the other hand, consist of sources utilized in project preparation, encompassing materials on the project's main subject, the individual's life or works, as well as any relevant aspects derived from them.

This distinction facilitates easy differentiation among sources.
