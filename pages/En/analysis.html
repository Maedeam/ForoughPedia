Capta

   

"The gathered datasets, are the essential basis for creating the visual representations in the project. In this section, the important details about the datasets are provided, including their descriptions, the planning decisions made, and insights into the methods and tools utilized during the compilation process. But why this section is titled Capta instead of Data? To emphasize a key idea and concept that significantly impacted the work. This concept is commonly used in Digital Humanities, as defined by Johanna Drucker: https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johanna_Drucker ""Capta is “taken” actively while data is assumed to be a “given” able to be recorded and observed. From this distinction, a world of differences arises. Humanistic inquiry acknowledges the situated, partial, and constitutive character of knowledge production, the recognition that knowledge is constructed, taken, not simply given as a natural representation of pre-existing fact. (Drucker, 2011)"" To visually show something, there is a need to turn it into numbers and tables. Think of it like translating—it involves making choices, but the analyses in this project are the result of a humanities-focused research, not just applying a statistical algorithm, but also they connect with processes of reading and interpreting. اینجا باید درباره دیتایی که آماده کردیم بگیم مثلا بگیم که متن هر دفتر شعر را بر اساس بیشترین کلمات استفاده شده تحلیل کردیم و بعد این کلمات را بر اساس مفهوم و تمی که به شعر میداد تحلیل کردیم و بعد مثلا نتیجه گرفتیم که خب الان توی این دفتر شعر فروغ بیشتر به فلان موضع پرداخته. البته... البته... می‌تونیم این کار رو اینجا نکنیم. اصلا بهتره اینجا نکنیم. و قبل از هر نموداری این توضیحاتش رو بنویسیم. و اینجا رو در نهایت برگردیم و ببینم چی لازمه. الان ادامه‌ش ندیم. بعدا حتما باید برگردیم متن رو بخونیم مثلش توضیح بدیم. دیره. "
