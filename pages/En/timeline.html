A Brief Life Timeline of Forough



1934, December 28
Birthday

Forough was born on January 15, 1313 in Tehran. Although according to the BBC, all sources consider this date to be her birthday, her sister Pooran Farrokhzad announced that the correct date is the 8th of December.

1947
The beginning of poetry

According to her sister Puran, she became interested in literature at the age of 11. She was writing poems in the form of Qazals (kinde of ode) by the age of 15, which were never published. Later, Forough referred to this period as the time when she had a "Qazal complex".

1951
Marriage

When she was sixteen, Forough left the conservatory school in 1329 because of love and married Parviz Shapour, the grandson of his mother's aunt, despite her family's opposition. Parviz was a finance employee eleven years older than Forough.

1953
Publication of the first poetry collection: Asir (The Captive)

Her first collection of poems was published in the same year her son Kamiyar was born. Asir, her first book, was published with an introduction by Shojauddin Shafa who asked readers to examine it without any moral judgment since like Latin American poetry and especially Alfonsina Storni's, her poems deal more with feelings than with intellect.

1954
Moving to Ahvaz (a ciyt in the south of Iran)

In 1332, Forough's family moved to Ahvaz due to Parviz's job.

1955
Divorce

Their short marriage ended in separation in 1334.

1956
Publication of the second poetry collection: Divar (The Wall)

The second volume of Forough's poetry was published in 1335 under the name Divar. In the same year, she traveled to Europe to overcome the nervous crises she was faced. During this trip, she spent a few months in Italy and Germany and learned the Italian and German languages ​​and translated poems from the poets of these two countries into Persian.

1957
Publication of the third poetry collection: Esyan (Rebellion)

In 1336, Forough published the poetry book of Rebellion.

1958
Beginning of cinema activities

Collaborating with Ebrahim Golestan as an assistant and editor for five episodes of the documentary films named Perspectives

1959
Travel to England

Collaborating with Ebrahim Golestan as an assistant and editor for five episodes of the documentary films named Perspectives

1960
Editing documentary films

Collaborating with Ebrahim Golestan as an assistant and editor for five episodes of the documentary films named Perspectives

1961
Numerous cinematic activities

- Traveling to Abadan in the company of Ebrahim Golestan and Suleiman Minassian to record the scenes related to the movie "Landscape of Water and Fire".
- Acting in the short film Khastagari (Courtship) directed by Ebrahim Golestan.
- Cooperation in the dubbing of The Seventh Seal under the supervision of xxxParviz Bahram.
- Acting in the movie Darya (The sea) directed by Ebrahim Golestan.
- Making a one-minute promotional film for Kaihan Institute.
- Being sent to England to participate in training courses related to the production and editing of documentary films.

1962
Production of one of the most influential documentaries in Iranian cinema

In the summer of 1941, Forough's first trip to Tabriz leprosy house (Baba Baghi) happened to learn about the condition of lepers and to start making of the documentary film The House Is Black, which was shown for the first time on February 19th, 1963, at the 82nd meeting of the Film Center. In the same year, Forough acted in the play "Six Characters in Search of an Author" by Luigi Pirandello, translated and directed by Pari Saberi.

1964
Receiving the Oberhausen Festival Award

- Receiving the award for the best documentary film from the Oberhausen International Film Festival for the film Black House - In a letter to her brother, she informed about writing a screenplay about the truth of Iranian women's life. She wrote that this scenario has 1000 pages, but it has not been published yet. - In 1963-64, Forough attempted suicide once. She took a box of Gardenal tablets, but her maid found out in the evening and took her to Alborz Hospital.

1965
Publication of the forth poetry collection: Another Birth

- Traveling to Germany, Italy and France - Acting in the movie Clay and Mirror directed by Ebrahim Golestan - Publishing the fourth collection of poems: Another Birth.

1966
Interview with Bernardo Bertolucci

- Interviewing with Italian director Bernardo Bertolucci
- In 1344, UNESCO produced a short documentary film about Forough's life."

1967, February 14
Death in 14 February

- Publication of Forough's poems in Germany, Sweden, England and France.
- She died on 24 February due to an accident which was suspected to be a murder.
